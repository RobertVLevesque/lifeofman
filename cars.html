The Canadian Ghost with 1,500 Horsepower: A 1966 Pontiac Beaumont Reborn

There are muscle cars… and then there are machines that feel like they’ve slipped through a crack in time—equal parts heritage, rebellion, and mechanical insanity. This twin-turbocharged 1966 Pontiac Beaumont, currently offered by August Motorcars, is firmly in the latter category.

It doesn’t just rewrite the rules of classic performance—it erases them entirely.

A Forgotten Canadian Icon

Before we get to the violence lurking under the hood, it’s worth understanding the canvas.

The Beaumont is one of those beautifully obscure chapters in automotive history—a Canadian-market creation born from General Motors’ need to satisfy domestic production rules in the 1960s. Built between 1966 and 1969, it blended Chevrolet underpinnings with Pontiac styling cues, essentially becoming a uniquely Canadian hybrid of Chevelle muscle and GTO attitude.

In its purest form, the Beaumont was never meant to be outrageous. It was subtle. Understated. A quiet insider’s muscle car.

This one? Not so much.

The Heart of a Monster

Let’s be clear: this is not a restoration.

This is a declaration of war.

Under the hood sits a twin-turbocharged 502 cubic-inch (8.2L) big-block V8, meticulously built and force-fed by a pair of turbos. The result? Over 1,500 horsepower, dyno-proven.

That kind of power doesn’t belong in 1966. Frankly, it barely belongs in 2026.

Backing it up is a T56 Magnum 6-speed manual, sending power to a bulletproof Ford 9-inch rear end. This isn’t just about brute force—it’s engineered brutality, the kind you can actually control… assuming your right foot has discipline.

Built, Not Restored

The real story here is the build quality.

This Beaumont rides on a modern Art Morrison Max G chassis, paired with C5 Corvette suspension components and a triangulated four-link rear.

Translation? It handles like something born decades later—because, in many ways, it was.

Braking is handled by Wilwood performance discs, hiding behind aggressive modern wheels. Grip is massive. Stance is perfect. Nothing about this car is accidental.

A Global Interior in a Canadian Body

Then you step inside… and the story shifts again.

This isn’t a time capsule—it’s a curated collision of automotive royalty:

  • Aston Martin interior elements
  • Audi R8 mirrors
  • Recaro performance seats (heated, ventilated, power-adjustable)
  • A hidden roll cage, integrated cleanly into the cabin

It’s part muscle car, part European grand tourer, part track weapon.
And somehow, it works.

The Philosophy of Excess

Cars like this exist in a strange space.

  • They aren’t restorations.
  • They aren’t replicas.
  • They aren’t even restomods in the traditional sense.

They are reinterpretations—what happens when you take a historically overlooked platform and ask:

“What if this had no limits?”

The answer is sitting here in gloss black, idling with the quiet menace of something that knows exactly how dangerous it is.

Price of Madness

Listed at roughly $300,000 CAD, this Beaumont isn’t cheap—but it’s also not trying to be.
Because you’re not just buying a car.

You’re buying:

  • A one-off build from a top-tier fabricator
  • A 1,500 horsepower statement piece
  • A slice of Canadian automotive history—rewritten

Final Verdict

In a world flooded with predictable builds—LS swaps, bolt-ons, cookie-cutter restorations—this Beaumont feels like a punch in the chest.

It’s rare.
It’s unapologetic.
It’s wildly overbuilt.

And most importantly… it’s unforgettable.

This isn’t just a car you drive.

IT'S A CAR YOU SURVIVE.
